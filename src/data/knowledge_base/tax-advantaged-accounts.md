# Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Tax-advantaged accounts are accounts that receive special tax treatment. Examples can include workplace retirement plans, traditional IRAs, Roth IRAs, health savings accounts, and education savings accounts.

The rules vary by account type. Some accounts may offer tax deductions, tax-deferred growth, or tax-free qualified withdrawals.

This topic is educational only. Account eligibility, contribution limits, and tax consequences can change and should be checked with current official guidance or a qualified tax professional.
