# Risk Tolerance

Risk tolerance describes how much uncertainty, volatility, and potential loss an investor can handle. It is shaped by time horizon, financial stability, goals, experience, and emotional comfort.

A long time horizon can sometimes support more stock exposure, while a short time horizon may call for more conservative assets. The right balance is personal and should be revisited over time.

Risk tolerance is not the same as risk capacity. Someone may feel comfortable with risk but still have limited ability to absorb losses.
