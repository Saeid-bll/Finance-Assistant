# Expense Ratios

An expense ratio is the annual cost charged by a mutual fund or ETF, expressed as a percentage of assets. A fund with a 0.10 percent expense ratio costs about one dollar per year for every one thousand dollars invested.

Lower costs can leave more of the return for the investor, especially over long periods. Costs are only one factor, though. Investors should also review holdings, risks, tracking, taxes, and fit with their goals.
