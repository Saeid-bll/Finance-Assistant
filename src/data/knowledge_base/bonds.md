# Bonds

A bond is a loan made to a government, company, or other issuer. In return, the issuer generally promises interest payments and repayment of principal at maturity.

Bonds are often used to reduce portfolio volatility, but they still have risks. Interest rate changes, inflation, credit quality, and maturity can affect bond prices.

Bond funds are diversified baskets of bonds, but their prices can also fall.
