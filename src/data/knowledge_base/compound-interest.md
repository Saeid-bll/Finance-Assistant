# Compound Interest

Compound interest means earning returns on both the original amount invested and on prior returns. Over long periods, compounding can make time in the market an important part of wealth building.

For beginners, the key idea is that small, regular contributions can grow meaningfully when they have enough time to compound. The result still depends on market returns, fees, taxes, and investor behavior.

Compounding is not guaranteed. Investments can lose value, especially over short periods.
