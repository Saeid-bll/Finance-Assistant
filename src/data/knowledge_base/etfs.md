# Exchange-Traded Funds

An exchange-traded fund, or ETF, is an investment fund that trades on an exchange like a stock. Many ETFs hold a basket of stocks, bonds, or other assets.

Broad market ETFs can offer diversification in a single ticker. Investors still need to understand the fund's holdings, costs, risks, and tax treatment.

ETF prices can move throughout the trading day, and the market value of ETF shares can rise or fall.
