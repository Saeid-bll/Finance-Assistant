# Emergency Fund

An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses or income disruptions. It is usually kept in safer, liquid accounts rather than invested in volatile assets.

Many educational resources discuss keeping several months of essential expenses available, but the right amount depends on job stability, dependents, insurance, debts, and personal comfort.

An emergency fund can help investors avoid selling long-term investments during a market downturn.
